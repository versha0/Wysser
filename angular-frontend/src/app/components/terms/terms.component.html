Terms and Conditions




Who can use these Terms & Conditions?

These Wysser terms and conditions are designed for use by a small business offering a delivery service for goods.



What are these Terms & Conditions for?

These terms and conditions set out the duties of both the transportation company and the retailer.



What are the main issues?

Customer obligations: the customer has to identify the goods being consigned and confirm that they’re not hazardous or perishable and that the customer owns them or has full authority to deal with the goods.

The terms will also deal with collection and delivery times: the goods must be ready for collection at the agreed time but the carrier will usually give only an estimate of the delivery time and not a guaranteed commitment.

The T&Cs will provide for a quotation to be given by the carrier which will be valid for a specified period

Charges may be varied in certain circumstances and if the customer cancels, there may be a cancellation fee.

Carriage will often be at the customer’s risk and, in that case, the customer has to arrange insurance of the goods in transit.

Carriers like to limit liability as much as they can so that they have no responsibility for loss or damage which is beyond their control.
